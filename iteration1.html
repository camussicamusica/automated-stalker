The Scoring Rubric

The 7 Scoring Dimensions

Each of the 28 candidates was scored 0–10 across seven dimensions, then multiplied by its weight. Weights were calibrated to Nuno's specific competitive threat profile.

Dimension 01
25%

Niche Density

Measures: How precisely the creator occupies Nuno's specific niche — AI automation + Python + business workflows.

Highest weight because exact-niche creators are maximally threatening regardless of audience size.

Dimension 02
25%

Webinar Strategy

Measures: Whether the creator uses webinars, live events, or cohort launches as a primary monetization channel.

Webinar competitors steal Nuno's audience during launch windows. Zero-webinar creators are irrelevant to launch strategy.

Dimension 03
20%

Audience Overlap

Measures: Shared subscriber demographics — small business owners, solopreneurs, and no-code/low-code adopters.

Perfect niche fit but zero audience overlap means not a real competitor for Nuno's specific buyers.

Dimension 04
10%

Content Format

Measures: Alignment with tutorial-style, long-form video content — Nuno's core format on YouTube.

Format divergence (short-form only, podcast-first) reduces competitive pressure even if niches overlap.

Dimension 05
10%

Offer Structure

Measures: Whether the creator sells courses, communities, templates, or SaaS competing with Nuno's launch.

Offer similarity determines whether the competitor will be positioned as an alternative at checkout.

Dimension 06
5%

Revenue Scale

Measures: Estimated monthly revenue from communities, courses, sponsorships, and SaaS.

Revenue scale signals market validation, not direct threat. Low weight avoids over-indexing on big names.

Dimension 07
5%

Authority Score

Measures: Domain authority, press coverage, backlink profile, and industry recognition signals.

Authority affects SEO and credibility halo — important context but not a direct proxy for threat to a launch.

Honest Methodology Note

⚠ Transparency Notice

The automated web scraping phase (report2.py) returned limited results — most websites blocked requests, Instagram and LinkedIn rejected scrapers, and Meta Ad Library is JavaScript-rendered and inaccessible to standard HTTP clients.

Individual candidate cards reflect only publicly accessible data (YouTube API metadata, publicly indexed pages). Real deep intelligence was only achievable on the Top 3 finalists using Claude Code + RivalSearchMCP with Cloudflare bypass, which enabled full website rendering, ad library access, community pricing visibility, and revenue estimation.

The 28-candidate cards are algorithmic signals, not verified profiles. The Top 3 reports are high-confidence intelligence.
