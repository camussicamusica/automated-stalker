Section 01

Why Random Research Fails

Without a systematic algorithm, competitor research suffers from confirmation bias, cherry-picking, and surface-level analysis that misses the real strategic threats.

🎯

Confirmation Bias

Manual research gravitates toward already-known names, missing dark-horse competitors who may pose greater strategic risk.

📊

No Weighting System

Without weighted dimensions, a creator with 2M subscribers but zero webinar overlap scores the same as a direct competitor with 50K followers.

🔀

Inconsistent Criteria

Switching criteria mid-analysis means you're not comparing apples to apples. The algorithm forces the same rubric for all 28 candidates.

⏳

Scale Impossibility

Deep-diving 28 creators manually would take weeks. Algorithmic scoring collapses that to hours, then reserves human intelligence for the Top 3.

The solution: A Darwinian algorithm that applies the same multi-dimensional scoring rubric to all 28 candidates, then eliminates the weakest performers across three successive generations — just as natural selection removes the unfit from a population. What survives is the fittest set of strategic competitors.

Section 03

The 7 Scoring Dimensions

Each of the 28 candidates was scored 0–10 across seven dimensions, then multiplied by its weight. Total scores range from 0–100. Weights were calibrated to Nuno's specific competitive threat profile.

Dimension 01
25%

Niche Density

Measures: How precisely the creator occupies Nuno's specific niche — AI automation + Python + business workflows.

Highest weight because a creator who exactly mirrors Nuno's positioning is maximally threatening regardless of size.

Dimension 02
25%

Webinar Strategy

Measures: Whether the creator uses webinars, live events, or cohort launches as a primary monetization channel.

Webinar competitors steal Nuno's audience during launch windows. Zero-webinar creators are irrelevant to the launch strategy.

Dimension 03
20%

Audience Overlap

Measures: Shared subscriber demographics — small business owners, solopreneurs, and no-code/low-code adopters.

A creator with perfect niche fit but zero audience overlap is not a real competitor for Nuno's specific buyers.

Dimension 04
10%

Content Format

Measures: Alignment with tutorial-style, long-form video content — Nuno's core format on YouTube.

Format divergence (e.g., short-form only, podcast-first) reduces competitive pressure even if niche overlaps.

Dimension 05
10%

Offer Structure

Measures: Whether the creator sells courses, communities, templates, or SaaS — products that compete with Nuno's launch.

Offer similarity determines whether the competitor will be positioned as an alternative to Nuno at checkout.

Dimension 06
5%

Revenue Scale

Measures: Estimated monthly revenue from all channels — community fees, courses, sponsorships, SaaS.

Revenue scale signals market validation, not direct threat. Low weight avoids over-indexing on big names that aren't real competitors.

Dimension 07
5%

Authority Score

Measures: Domain authority, press coverage, backlink profile, and industry recognition signals.

Authority affects SEO and credibility halo — important context but not a direct proxy for competitive threat to a launch.

Section 05

Honest Methodology Note

⚠ Transparency Notice

The automated web scraping phase (report2.py) returned limited results — most websites blocked requests, Instagram and LinkedIn rejected scrapers, and Meta Ad Library is JavaScript-rendered and inaccessible to standard HTTP clients.

Individual candidate cards reflect only publicly accessible data (YouTube API metadata, publicly indexed pages). Real deep intelligence was only achievable on the Top 3 finalists using Claude Code + RivalSearchMCP with Cloudflare bypass, which enabled full website rendering, ad library access, community pricing visibility, and revenue estimation.

The 28-candidate cards are algorithmic signals, not verified profiles. The Top 3 reports are high-confidence intelligence.
