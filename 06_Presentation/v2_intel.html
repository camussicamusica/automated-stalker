What it is

RivalSearchMCP is an open-source Model Context Protocol (MCP) server built by Damion Rashford. It gives AI assistants like Claude a set of real-time web tools — search, social scraping, website mapping, and research aggregation — that the model can call directly during a conversation. No browser required. No manual copy-pasting.

How MCP works

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol — an open standard by Anthropic that lets external tools "plug in" to a language model's context window. Think of it like browser extensions, but for AI. When Claude Code has RivalSearchMCP connected, it can call tools like web_search, map_website, and research_topic mid-session, retrieve live data, and incorporate it into its response — all in one uninterrupted flow.

Strategic Analysis for Nuno Tavares

Based on the intelligence above, here are the competitive gaps, competitor weaknesses, and key lessons applicable to Nuno's positioning as @AutomatedMarketer.

Competitive Gaps in the Market
Gap #1

GHL + AI + Organic YouTube — The Unfilled Slot

Liam Ottley dominates organic YouTube with 730K subs but has NO GHL connection. Jordan Platten has GHL but only 375K subs and a UK-centric audience. Billy Gene has the strongest GHL relationship but relies heavily on paid ads and targets over-40 professionals. The gap: a strong YouTube presence that combines AI automation + GHL education for the 25–40 entrepreneur crowd, with organic-first strategy.

Gap #2

Transparent Pricing vs. "Book a Call"

All 3 competitors hide their high-ticket pricing behind application funnels. This creates friction and distrust for a portion of the market. A competitor willing to be more transparent about pricing could attract buyers who disengage from the hidden-price model.

Gap #3

AI + GHL SaaS Reseller Model

None of these 3 clearly dominates the space of "use GHL as your white-label SaaS product, automated with AI." This combines Liam's AI-build angle with Jordan's/Billy's GHL affiliation — and has high recurring revenue potential for students.

Competitor Weaknesses
🥇 Liam Ottley — Weaknesses
  • Instagram dead zone: 33K followers, 11 posts — no short-form content engine. Missing an entire acquisition channel.
  • No GHL integration: Missing the largest affiliate revenue opportunity in the agency training space.
  • $5K–$7K price point competition: Multiple similar-priced offers entering the market.
  • Refund policy issues: Criticized on Reddit and in YouTube reviews — potential churn and reputation risk.
🥈 Jordan Platten — Weaknesses
  • UK-centric audience: Limits US market penetration — biggest addressable market.
  • Hidden pricing friction: Agency Launch pricing ($3.8K–$4.5K) not publicly displayed — creates friction with sophisticated buyers.
  • Smaller YouTube channel: 375K vs Liam's 730K — 2x disadvantage in organic reach.
  • "Agency Giants" podcast: Under-branded and under-leveraged given his audience size.
🥉 Billy Gene — Weaknesses
  • YouTube severely underutilized: Only 31 videos in current playlist — leaving organic search and evergreen content value on the table.
  • Over-reliance on paid ads: Business is tied to Facebook/Meta ad costs and platform risk.
  • "Over 40" targeting is narrowing: Excludes large younger entrepreneur market (25–40 demographic).
  • Scattered funnel: Multiple URL variants for the same product (5+ accelerator URLs) suggests brand confusion and untested conversion paths.
Key Lessons from Top Performers
From Liam Ottley
  • FREE community as core funnel asset is extraordinarily powerful — 280K+ free members converting to high-ticket is a proven three-step machine.
  • Owning a real agency gives unmatched credibility ("I practice what I teach") and differentiates from pure educators.
  • YouTube → Skool → High-ticket: Clean, simple, scalable funnel architecture that doesn't require paid ads to sustain.
From Jordan Platten
  • GHL affiliation creates passive recurring revenue and deep curriculum integration — this should be explored by any agency educator building on the GHL ecosystem.
  • CPD accreditation (or equivalent credentialing) adds a significant trust signal — especially for buyers comparing multiple programs.
  • Cross-platform distribution (YouTube + Facebook + LinkedIn + Instagram) maximizes audience reach and resilience against any single platform algorithm change.
From Billy Gene
  • Low-ticket entry offers ($97–$197) dramatically expand the funnel top and identify buyers early, making high-ticket backend sales more efficient.
  • Facebook/Instagram as primary channels reach audiences that YouTube doesn't — especially professionals over 35 with budget.
  • Having your OWN branded AI tool (billygenesai.com) is a future-proof competitive moat — it creates stickiness, recurring revenue, and brand identity no competitor can easily replicate.
  • Podcast with 2M listeners is a massive, underappreciated distribution asset that compounds brand authority over time.
