STRATEGIC ANALYSIS FOR NUNO TAVARES (@AutomatedMarketer)

================================================================================

1. COMPETITIVE GAPS IN THE MARKET

   a) GHL + AI + Organic YouTube (Unfilled Slot):
      Liam Ottley dominates organic YouTube with 730K subs but has NO GHL
      connection. Jordan Platten has GHL but only 375K subs and a UK-centric
      audience. Billy Gene has the strongest GHL relationship but relies
      heavily on paid ads and targets over-40 professionals.
      THE GAP: A strong YouTube presence that combines AI automation + GHL
      education for the 25-40 entrepreneur crowd, with organic-first strategy.

   b) Transparent Pricing vs. "Book a Call":
      All 3 competitors hide their high-ticket pricing behind application
      funnels. This creates friction and distrust for a portion of the market.
      A competitor willing to be more transparent about pricing could attract
      buyers who disengage from the hidden-price model.

   c) The AI + GHL SaaS Reseller Model:
      None of these 3 clearly dominates the space of "use GHL as your
      white-label SaaS product, automated with AI." This is a growing niche
      that combines Liam's AI-build angle with Jordan's/Billy's GHL affiliation
      — and it has high recurring revenue potential for students.

2. COMPETITOR WEAKNESSES

   Liam Ottley:
   - Instagram is essentially dead (33K, 11 posts) — no short-form content engine
   - No GHL integration means missing the largest affiliate revenue opportunity
     in the agency training space
   - $5K-$7K price point is becoming competitive (multiple similar-priced offers)
   - Refund policy criticized on Reddit and in YouTube reviews

   Jordan Platten:
   - UK-centric audience limits US market penetration
   - Agency Launch pricing ($3.8K-$4.5K) is NOT publicly displayed — friction
   - Relatively smaller YouTube channel (375K) vs Liam (730K)
   - "Agency Giants" podcast is not as well-branded as it could be

   Billy Gene:
   - YouTube is severely underutilized (only 31 videos in current playlist)
   - Over-reliance on paid ads means business is tied to Facebook/Meta ad costs
   - "Over 40" targeting is narrowing — excludes large younger entrepreneur market
   - Multiple URL variants for the same product (5+ accelerator URLs) suggests
     scattered funnel strategy and brand confusion

3. KEY LESSONS FROM TOP PERFORMERS

   From Liam Ottley:
   - FREE community as core funnel asset is extraordinarily powerful
   - YouTube → Skool → High-ticket is a proven three-step machine
   - Owning a real agency gives unmatched credibility ("I practice what I teach")

   From Jordan Platten:
   - GHL affiliation creates passive recurring revenue and deep curriculum
     integration — this should be explored by any agency educator
   - CPD accreditation (or equivalent credentialing) adds trust signal
   - Cross-platform distribution (YouTube + Facebook + LinkedIn + Instagram)
     maximizes audience reach

   From Billy Gene:
   - Low-ticket entry offers ($97-$197) dramatically expand the funnel top
   - Facebook/Instagram as primary channels reach audiences YouTube doesn't
   - Having your OWN branded AI tool is a future-proof moat
   - Podcast with 2M listeners is a massive, underappreciated distribution asset
